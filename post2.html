APA vs MLA vs Chicago: A Quick Style Guide

person Brian Ogot, Founder- Smart Essay Coach   |   event July 5, 2025

Choosing the right citation style is essential for academic writing success. Whether you're writing essays, research papers, or dissertations, understanding the differences between APA, MLA, and Chicago styles will save you time and improve the professionalism of your work. Here's a quick but thorough guide to help you format your papers confidently.

style APA Style (American Psychological Association)

APA style is widely used in the social sciences. It emphasizes author-date citations within the text, for example, (Smith, 2020). The reference list at the end is titled “References” and includes full publication details. APA focuses on clarity, precision, and the date of publication to highlight recent research.

style MLA Style (Modern Language Association)

MLA style is common in humanities disciplines like literature and cultural studies. It uses author-page format for in-text citations, such as (Smith 23). The final list is called “Works Cited.” MLA focuses on ease of reading and straightforward attribution of sources.

style Chicago Style (Chicago Manual of Style)

Chicago style is versatile and used in history and many other fields.
