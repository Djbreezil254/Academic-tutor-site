APA vs MLA vs Chicago: A Quick Style Guide

Choosing the right citation style can be confusing. Here’s a quick breakdown of APA, MLA, and Chicago to help you format your papers with confidence.

APA Style (American Psychological Association)

Common in social sciences, APA emphasizes author-date citations. It uses in-text citations like (Smith, 2020) and includes a “References” page at the end.

MLA Style (Modern Language Association)

Popular in the humanities, MLA uses author-page citations such as (Smith 23). The “Works Cited” section concludes the paper.

Chicago Style (Chicago Manual of Style)
