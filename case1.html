Case Study: Guiding an RN-BSN Student to Success in UTA’s NURS 4465

Discover how Smart Essay Coach helped Jane, a working RN in UTA’s RN-BSN program, overcome challenges in NURS 4465 Population and Community Health Nursing to achieve a 93% final grade.

Start Your Success Story

report_problem The Challenge

Jane, a full-time registered nurse in North Dallas, was enrolled in the University of Texas at Arlington’s online RN-BSN program, specifically the NURS 4465 Population and Community Health Nursing course. Juggling work and studies, she found the asynchronous course format overwhelming. The multi-part assignments, including a windshield survey, statistical analysis, and community health intervention, were complex. Jane struggled with initial discussion posts, missed a quiz, and started with a 75% average grade. Demoralized and worried about passing, she sought Smart Essay Coach to improve her performance and aim for an A.

lightbulb Our Solution

Smart Essay Coach provided Jane with personalized, holistic coaching tailored to NURS 4465’s requirements. Our approach included:

  • Syllabus Analysis: We reviewed the course syllabus, clarifying learning outcomes like community assessments and research integration, and emphasized the grading structure (C or better to pass) and late policies (5-point deduction per day, quizzes not accepted late).
  • Assignment Guidance: For Assignment 1 (Windshield Survey & Key Informant Interview), we coached Jane on observing her North Dallas community and interviewing key informants (e.g., a local librarian) with targeted questions. For Assignment 2 (Comparative Statistical Analysis), we guided her to credible sources like CDC for population health data and helped correlate subjective and objective data to develop Community Health Nursing Diagnoses. For Assignment 3 (Community Health Nursing Intervention), we supported her in creating a care plan by synthesizing prior assignments.
  • APA and Writing Skills: We ensured all assignments adhered to APA 7th edition guidelines, teaching Jane proper citation for peer-reviewed journals from UTA’s Nursing library page, avoiding penalties from the Office of Community Standards.
  • Time Management: We created a schedule for discussion posts (initial posts due Wednesdays, replies by Saturdays) and quiz reminders, preventing further missed deadlines.

star The Results

With consistent coaching, Jane transformed her performance. She completed all assignments with strong research, clear writing, and correct APA formatting. Her strategic approach to discussion posts and quizzes eliminated penalties. By the end of NURS 4465, Jane achieved a final grade of 93% (an A), a significant leap from her initial 75%. She gained confidence in community health concepts, honed her academic writing skills, and built a foundation for success in her RN-BSN program and nursing career.
